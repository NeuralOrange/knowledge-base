# Why DDD Matters More in the AI Era

<!-- more -->

When AI generates code, what's left for humans? **Understanding the domain.**

Domain-Driven Design was created to solve the communication gap between
developers and domain experts. In the AI era, this gap becomes a chasm —
AI doesn't know your business unless you tell it.

## The Argument

1. AI can write code, but it can't understand your business
2. Ubiquitous Language becomes the interface between humans, AI, and code
3. Bounded Contexts help AI agents stay within scope
4. Domain Events are perfect for AI agent orchestration

## What Changes with AI

The *tactical* patterns of DDD (aggregates, repositories, entities) become
less important — AI can implement those. But the *strategic* patterns
(bounded contexts, ubiquitous language, context mapping) become **more**
important — they're how we communicate intent.

## My Take

DDD was always about communication. AI makes communication more critical,
not less. If you can't explain your domain clearly, AI can't help you build it.
